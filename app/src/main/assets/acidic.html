Identification of Acidic Radicals

Dilute hydrochloric acid is added to the dry substance and the gas given off is identified.

Na2CO3 +2NaCl→ 2NaCl+H2O +CO2

Concentrated tetraoxosulphate (VI) acid can also be used in identifying the acidic radical .The acid is carefully poured onto the substance in a test tube and heated gently, if no reaction occurs:

Na2CO3+ H2SO4 → Na2SO4+ H2O +CO2

The action of heat, dilute hydrochloric acid or concentrated tetraoxosulphate (VI) acid on the substance gives an indication as to the acidic radical present.

Test: Action of Heat

Heat a little of the dry substance in a test tube until no further change is seen. Note the state of the residue. The following Observation and Inference give a guide.

Observation

Colourless, odourless gas, rekindles a glowing splint.

Inference

Oxygen from oxides

Observation

Colourless, odourless gas, turn lime water milky

Inference

Carbon(IV) oxide from carbonates and hydrogen carbonates

Observation

Liquid condenses on sides of test tube and turns anhydrous copper tetraoxosulphate(VI) blue

Inference

Water of crystallization from hydrated salt

Observation

Reddish brown fumes

Inference

Nitrogen(IV) oxide

Observation

Yellow Substance when hot and white when cold

Inference

Zinc salt

Observation

Dark red substance when hot and yellow when cold

Inference

Lead salt

To the dry substance in a test tube, add some dilute hydrochloric acid and warm gently, if no reaction occurs.

Observation

If a reddish brown gas is given off

Inference

Gas is nitrogen (IV) oxide which indicates presence of trioxonitrate or dioxonitrate(III)

Observation

If a colourless, pungent gas which turns acidified potassium heptaoxodichromate (VI) paper green

Inference

Gas is sulphur(IV) oxide which indicate presence of trioxosulphate(IV) ion.

Observation

If gas smells of rotten eggs and blackens lead(II) ethanoate paper

Inference

Gas is hydrogen sulphide which indicates presence of sulphide ion

Observation

If gas turn lime water milky

Inference

Gas is carbon(IV) oxide which indicates presence of trioxocarbonate(IV) or hydrogen trioxocarbonate(IV) ion

To the dry substance, add a little concentrated tetraoxosulphate (VI) acid and warm gently, if no reaction occurs. Take careful note, this acid is very corrosive.

Observation

If gas turn lime water milky:

Inference

Gas is carbon(IV) oxide, indicating presence of trioxocarbonate (IV) or hydrogen trioxocarbonate(IV) ion.

Observation

If a white precipitate is formed:

Inference

The precipitate is most likely lead tetraxosulphate(VI) formed from the reaction between the acid and the lead salt.

H2SO4 + PbX → PbSO4 + H2X

Observation

If a colourless gas which fumes in moist air is given off:

Inference

Gas is hydrogen chloride, indicating presence of chloride ion.

Observation

If brown fumes which become deep brown on addition of copper turnings are observed:

Inference

Gas is nitrogen(IV) oxide, indicating presence of trioxocarbonate (IV) ion.
